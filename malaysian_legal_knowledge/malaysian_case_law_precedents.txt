CONSTITUTIONAL LAW PRECEDENTS:

Loh Kooi Choon v Government of Malaysia [1977] 2 MLJ 187
HELD: Article 5(1) of the Federal Constitution guarantees liberty of person
PRINCIPLE: Preventive detention must be justified under specific constitutional provisions

PP v Dato Yap Peng [1987] 2 MLJ 311  
HELD: Burden of proof in criminal cases - prosecution must prove beyond reasonable doubt
PRINCIPLE: Standard of proof in Malaysian criminal law

EMPLOYMENT LAW PRECEDENTS:

Wong Yuen Hock v Syarikat Hong Leong Assurance Sdn Bhd [1995] 2 MLJ 753
HELD: Constructive dismissal - employer's conduct makes employment untenable
PRINCIPLE: Employee may treat contract as terminated due to employer's breach

Goon Kwee Phoy v J&P Coats (M) Sdn Bhd [1981] 2 MLJ 129
HELD: Just cause for dismissal under Employment Act 1955
PRINCIPLE: Misconduct must be serious enough to warrant dismissal

PRINCIPLE: Remedies for breach of commercial contracts
